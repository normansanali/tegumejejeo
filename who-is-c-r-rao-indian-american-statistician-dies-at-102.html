Who is C. R. Rao? Indian-American statistician dies at 102

 Posted on August 29, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  436 words  |  Martina Birk

The world of academia and scientific inquiry mourns the passing of a true visionary as Calyampudi Radhakrishna Rao, widely known as C. R. Rao, breathed his last on August 23, 2023, at the age of 102. This remarkable Indian-American mathematician and statistician leaves behind a legacy that has not only transformed his field but also left an indelible mark on a multitude of disciplines.

An Inspirational Life

C. R. Rao’s journey from a small town in India to becoming a renowned scholar is a story of perseverance, passion, and unparalleled dedication. Born on September 10, 1920, in Hadagali, Bellary, his academic pursuits took him on an incredible trajectory that shaped the world of mathematics and statistics.

A Global Scholar

During his illustrious career, C. R. Rao held esteemed positions as a professor emeritus at Pennsylvania State University and a Research Professor at the University at Buffalo. His contributions to the field were not only recognized through a multitude of honorary degrees, colloquia, and awards but also through his profound impact on various spheres of knowledge.

Pioneering Contributions

Described by the American Statistical Association as a “living legend,” C. R. Rao’s work extended far beyond the boundaries of statistics. His insights reverberated across diverse domains including economics, genetics, anthropology, geology, and even national planning and demography. His pioneering efforts have paved the way for advancements in biometry and medicine, making his legacy even more enduring.

A Global Acclaim

C. R. Rao’s stature as a preeminent scholar was further underscored by his recognition as one of the top 10 Indian scientists of all time by The Times of India. In 2002, he received the prestigious US National Medal of Science, a testament to the profound influence of his work. His remarkable contributions were once again celebrated in 2023, as he was awarded the International Prize in Statistics—a distinction often likened to the “statistics’ equivalent of the Nobel Prize.”

A Heartfelt Advocate

Beyond his scholarly pursuits, C. R. Rao was also a dedicated Senior Policy and Statistics Advisor for the Indian Heart Association. His commitment to raising awareness about cardiovascular diseases among South Asians showcased his multifaceted commitment to public health and societal well-being.

A Lasting Legacy

As the curtain falls on the remarkable life of C. R. Rao, his legacy remains an inspiration for future generations. His journey, from a small town to global recognition, is a testament to the power of human curiosity, intellect, and determination. While he may no longer be with us, C. R. Rao’s contributions to mathematics, statistics, and the world at large will continue to shape progress and understanding for years to come.
