Tyson Beckford biography

This is a brief Tyson Beckford biography just for you to know who he is if you have never heard about him and what he does.

He is an American fashion model and actor who has a net worth of $8 million.

Before making a shift to a film and television career, Beckford first rose to fame modelling for clothier Ralph Lauren, after which he earned the distinction of becoming the first African-American male supermodel.

In 1995, VH1 named Tyson “Man of the Year,” and he was featured in “People” magazine’s “50 Most Beautiful People in the World” list.

Several years later, he began appearing in films, such as “Zoolander” (2001), “Biker Boyz” (2003), and “Into the Blue” (2005).

Tyson Beckford early life

Further on Tyson Beckford biography, we look at his early life and how it was for him when he was growing up as a child.

He was born Tyson Craig Beckford on December 19, 1970, in Rochester, New York.

His mother is called Hillary, who is of Afro-Jamaican descent, and his father is Lloyd, who is of Chinese-Jamaican and Afro-Jamaican heritage.

The family moved to Jamaica when Tyson was very young and spent seven years there, then moved back to Rochester.

Beckford attended the top-ranked Pittsford Mendon High School in Pittsford where he played football for his High School.

Tyson Beckford career

From humble beginnings, how did Tyson Beckford career go?

Let’s talk about Tyson Beckford career now.

Tyson’s career has seen many interesting turns; from modelling to appearing in films and hosting television shows, he has been doing quite a lot.

In 1992, talent scout Jeff Jones discovered Beckford at NYC’s Washington Square Park and recruited him for “The Source” magazine.

The following year, he began modelling for Ralph Lauren’s Polo sportswear line.

Tyson signed with NYC’s Soul Artist Management and Milan’s D’management Group, and VH1 ranked him #38 on their “40 Hottest Hotties of the ’90s” list.

In 2008, Beckford began co-hosting “Make Me a Supermodel” with model Niki Taylor, and during season two, he was the show’s only host.

He also served as a judge on the Australian version of the series as well as “America’s Next Top Model” and “Britain and Ireland’s Next Top Model.”
