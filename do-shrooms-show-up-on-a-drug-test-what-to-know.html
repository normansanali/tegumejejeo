Do Shrooms Show Up On A Drug Test - What To Know?

Are you worried about magic mushrooms, also known as shrooms, showing up in a drug test? Many people smoke shrooms and the answer is that they are not simply detectable. While regular tests usually cannot detect shrooms, some specialized ones can see them. In this blog, we’ll talk about how long they stick around in your body, what affects detection, and if there is any way to get them out. Let’s explore the science behind do shrooms show up on a drug test and understand the factors that matter. Sit back and read on!

Do shrooms show up on a drug test?

Usually, shrooms won’t be able to detect on regular drug tests; however, according to the Drug and Alcohol Testing Industry Association, there are more specialized tests that can see them along with other hallucinogenic drugs like LSD, mescaline, and peyote.

These substances leave the body quite quickly, so these tests might be done in specific situations, such as when someone seems to be under the influence of these drugs at a particular time.It is important to note that there’s a potential risk of magic mushrooms being mixed with other detectable drugs. For example, if you buy mushrooms from an untrustworthy source, they might be contaminated with other substances that could show up on a drug test.

According to the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA), here are some commonly performed tests to detect the below-mentioned drugs respectively:

Urine test

Urine tests are one of the most commonly performed drug tests to screen 5 categories of drugs. It includes:

  • cannabis

  • cocaine

  • amphetamines

  • opiates

  • phencyclidine, or PCP

People also know this as a five-panel test. Other screenings might also include different drug testing categories, such as:

  • barbituates

  • alcohol

  • benzodiazepines

  • MDMA

  • propoxyphene

  • methadone

  • methaqualone

Hair follicle drug test: Hair follicle tests are less standard than urine tests. However, it is higher in cost. A hair follicle test could detect drugs for as long as 90 days.

How Long Do Shrooms Stay in Your System?

When you eat hallucinogenic mushrooms, your body breaks them down into active substances like psilocybin and psilocin, which affect your brain, leading to changes in how you feel and perceive things.

Are you finding the answer to “how long does shrooms stay in your system”.This process happens quite fast after smoking shrooms. After eating the mushrooms, you can feel different in just 20-30 minutes. The effects can last for several hours, depending on factors like how much you took and your response.

At the same time, your body starts getting rid of these substances. The kidneys remove them and pass them out of your body through urine.

Here’s how long it is detectable by a drug test

  • Around 66% of the substances are out of your body within 3 hours.

  • After 24 hours, they can no longer be detected in your urine.

There are various factors can affect these timings

  • Dosage: If you take more, it might stay in your body longer.

  • Mushroom potency: Stronger mushrooms may have more active substances, making the effects last longer.

  • Type of mushrooms: Different mushrooms have different compositions that can influence how long they stay in your body.

  • Personal tolerance: If you regularly take these substances, it might take more time for them to leave your system.

  • Preparation method: How you prepare the mushrooms (dried, in tea, etc.) can affect their strength and how quickly your body gets rid of them.

  • Food or drink with the mushrooms: Eating them with other foods or on an empty stomach can affect how long they stay in your body.

  • Metabolism: Your body’s natural processes, influenced by age and body composition, can affect how quickly these substances leave your system.

  • Kidney disorders: If your kidneys aren’t working well, it might take more time for your body to break down and get rid of these substances.

*Note: It is important to note that there’s researchstating that shrooms could be detected for up to a week.

What are the factors that may affect the detection?

Several factors can influence how long magic mushrooms (shrooms) stay in your system. Some of these factors are beyond your control.

1. Time Between Taking Shrooms and Testing:

The duration between consuming shrooms and undergoing a drug test matters. The sooner the test is conducted after taking shrooms, the more likely it is to detect them.

2. Type of Mushroom

There are many types of mushrooms containing the hallucinogen psilocybin. The amount of psilocybin varies between different mushrooms, affecting how long it stays in your body.

3. Method of Consumption

How you consume shrooms, whether dried, fresh, in food, or as tea, influences their potency and how quickly your body processes them.

4. Dosage

The quantity you consume also plays a role. Larger doses mean shrooms will stay in your system longer and may be detectable.

5. Age

Your metabolism, liver, and kidney functions slow down as you age. This can delay the removal of psilocybin from your body, making shrooms linger longer, especially in older individuals.

6. Individual Differences

Everyone’s body is unique. Factors like body mass index (BMI), metabolism, and water content can affect how quickly your body processes substances.

7. Stomach Contents

When you take shrooms, the amount of food and liquid in your stomach influences their duration in your system. A fuller stomach slows down the digestive process, while hydration speeds up the elimination of psilocybin.

8. Interaction with Other Substances

Using shrooms alongside other substances can have unpredictable effects and impact the time they stay in your system. Alcohol or other substances may affect how your body processes shrooms and could be detected in a drug test.

9. Possibility of Laced Shrooms

It’s essential to be aware that the shrooms you obtain might be mixed with other substances, potentially affecting both the experience and the duration they remain in your system.

Can you get them out of your system faster?

Not really! Drinking more water is the only way to detox your body faster and pass the unwanted substances through the urine. There’s no other way to get them out of your system more quickly from your body. It is better to avoid consuming shrooms if you are worried about the detection.

Conclusion

So, can you hide shrooms showing up in a drug test? Not really. We’ve covered the time it takes them to leave your body, how different factors play a role, and why you can’t speed up the process. This guide gives you enough information about “does shrooms come up on a drug test”and helps you make smart choices and know the risks. Remember, knowing the facts enables you to avoid these situations wisely. But, it is always advisable to avoid consuming shrooms in any way.
