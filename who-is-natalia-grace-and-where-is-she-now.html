The Barnetts: Natalia’s First Adoptive Parents

Natalia Grace’s initial adoptive parents were Michael and Kristine Barnett, who embraced her into their family in 2010 after adopting the Ukrainian orphan with dwarfism. The Barnetts stirred controversy when they asserted that Natalia was misrepresenting her age, alleging that she was an adult rather than a child. This dispute resulted in legal complications, charges of neglect, and a tumultuous relationship between the Barnetts and Natalia.

Legal Issues and Accusations

The Barnetts faced charges for leaving Natalia unattended in 2013, but these charges were ultimately dismissed. They accused Natalia of exhibiting violent behavior, which she vehemently denies. The accusations and legal battles further complicated Natalia’s life and garnered significant attention.

The Mans’ Adoption and the Documentary

In 2023, following the strained relationship between Natalia and the Barnetts, Cynthia and Antwon Mans became her adoptive parents. The Mans family featured prominently in the documentary series “The Curious Case of Natalia Grace: Natalia Speaks,” providing insights into their experiences and perspectives surrounding Natalia.

Natalia’s Early Life and Diagnosis

Born in Ukraine in 2003, Natalia Grace faced the challenges of living with spondyloepiphyseal dysplasia congenita, a rare form of dwarfism. Due to her condition, she became a difficult child and was placed in an orphanage. In 2010, the Barnetts adopted her, renaming her Natalia Grace Barnett.

Abandonment and Legal Battles

In 2013, the Barnetts departed for Canada, leaving Natalia alone in an apartment. This act of abandonment, coupled with subsequent legal disputes and a DNA test, brought to light the intricacies and challenges that Natalia confronted in her early years. These developments resulted in ongoing disputes and controversies.
