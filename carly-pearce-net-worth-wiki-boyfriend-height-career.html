How did Carly Pearce start her Professional Career?

At the age of 11, Carly Pearce started singing professionally. She frequently played in a bluegrass band and tent revivals and church performances. She also quit high school to join the “Country Crossroads” show at the age of 16, where she performed five times per week, and tried out for it.

This vocalist relocated to Nashville, Tennessee, when she was 19 years old to pursue a career in country music. She had a development deal with Sony Music Nashville in 2012 when she was 22 years old, but it was immediately terminated. She then took on a number of part-time jobs, including cleaning Airbnb.

After getting in touch with Pete Fisher, vice president and general manager of the Grand Ole Opry, in 2015, Carly performed at the venue.

She was given a developing artist deal by Busbee that same year, and later that year, she appeared on the song “Wasn’t That Drunk.”

After the release of her first single, “Every Little Thing,” in February 2017, her career took off. The song was a smash hit, peaking at number one on the Billboard Country Airplay list and number five on the Billboard Hot Country Songs chart. Her first studio album, “Every Little Thing,” was made available on October 13, 2017.

She later joined Luke Bryan, Thomas Rhett, Blake Shelton, and other country musicians on several tours. Later that year, she recorded “Hide the Wine,” her second big hit, which peaked at number 13 on the Billboard Country Airplay list.

Another song, “Closer to You,” was made available in November 2018. The second track from her unreleased second studio album, “I Hope You’re Happy Now,” was made public by the artist in October 2019.

On February 14, 2020, Carly Pearce will release her self-titled second studio album, which will chart at No. 6 on the Top Country Albums chart and No. 73 on the Billboard 200 chart.
