Career

Early Life

Born on July 30, 1971, Christine Taylor is the daughter of Joan, a homemaker, and Albert Taylor, who runs a security company. Namely, she attended Allentown Central Catholic High School.

Christine’s acting career began at an early age when she started acting in a children’s TV show called Hey Dude (1989), broadcasted on Nickelodeon. And, in between 1991 and 1992, she worked solely on television projects.

In the early 1990s, she gave many hit TV series like Blossom and Dallas, while in the late 1990s, she returned with other popular shows like Murphy Brown and Cupid.

However, even after doing some commercially successful movies, the actress continued working in television, taking small breaks in between.

To detail, the actress is popularly known for her acting in the movies like The Wedding Singer, Zoolander, DodgeBall: A True Underdog Story, and many more.

Personal Life

On May 13, 2000, Christine got married to the popular actor Ben Stiller and later blessed with two children, daughter Ella Olivia and a son Quinlin Dempsey.

The couple met each other during the shooting of the TV show Heat Vision and Jack.

In fact, Ben and Christine both have appeared onscreen together in Zoolander, DodgeBall: A True Underdog Story, Tropic Thunder, Zoolander 2, Curb Your Enthusiasm, and Arrested Development. At the same time, the couple also managed to raise two children while carrying their other projects.

After 18 years of marriage, the couple announced their separation in 2017, which took the world in shock. However, they appeared all smiles on Emmys Purple Carpet in 2019, posing together, which surely made all the fans happy.
