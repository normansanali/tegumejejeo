Marie Osmond | Career

Marie began appearing as a solo act at her brothers’ concerts, differentiating herself from her pop-obsessed siblings by concentrating solely on country music.

Osmond was persuaded to record an album by her mother. “Paper Roses,” Marie’s debut single, was released in 1973.

The track helped Marie gain fame as it reached number one on the country charts and five on the pop charts.

Marie also recorded several duets with her brother Donny at that time.

But by 1975, she had begun to lean more toward pop music with the release of the album “This is the Way That I Feel.”
