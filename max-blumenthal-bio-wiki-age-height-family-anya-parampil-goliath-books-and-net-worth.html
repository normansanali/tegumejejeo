Max Blumenthal Biography

Max Blumenthal is an American journalist, author, blogger, and filmmaker who was born on 18 December 1977 in Boston, Massachusetts, United States. Max set up an independent news website known as The Grayzone in December 2015 and he is the website’s editor and one of its contributors. He is also a regular contributor to Sputnik and RT.

Max Syria

In September 2013, Max reported for The Nation from the Zaatari refugee camp in Jordan on the conditions in which Syrian refugees were living. He wrote that he was against US strikes, mainly because he believed they could worsen an already horrific situation without changing the political reality in any meaningful way. Max also wrote that the refugees in Zaatari deserved to be heard and commented

According to Janine di Giovanni in The New York Review of Books, after Max’s visit to Moscow in December 2015, he started to advance views supportive of Bashar al Assad and the Syrian government’s position. Max, she wrote, was among a group of “Assad apologists.”After the December 2015 trip, he said the White Helmets were connected to Al-Qaeda and anti-Assad Syrians were representative of the group

In September 2019,  Max was part of an American representative who visited Damascus to take participate in an Assad-backed trade union convention and to oppose the economic blockade, imperialist interventions as well as terrorism

Max Goliath

Max’s Goliath, published by Nation Books, depicts what Max terms as Israel’s aggressive shift to the far-right, and its crackdown on local activism.   The book consists of 73 short chapters. Chapter titles include, To the Slaughter, The Concentration Camp, The Night of Broken Glass, This Belongs to the White Man, as well as, How to Kill Goyim and Influence People

Max Gaza

In The 51 Day War (2015), Max writes that he was in Gaza during and following Operation Protective Edge, the Israeli military offensive in Gaza during the summer of 2014. Max said that the catalyst for the military offensive was the kidnapping and killing of three Israeli teenagers by a Hamas cell. He declared that Israel’s West Bank operation was not aimed at saving the teens, who were known to be dead, or capturing their murderers but pulling down a political agreement between Hamas and the Palestinian National Authority by aiming at the Palestinian Unity Government.
