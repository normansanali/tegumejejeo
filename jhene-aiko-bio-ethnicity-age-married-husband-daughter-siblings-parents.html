Jhene Aiko Bio, Ethnicity, Age, Married, Husband, Daughter, Siblings, Parents

 Posted on August 4, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1083 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Jhené Aiko is an American singer and songwriter who started out as a contributing vocalist for the famous late ’90s and early 2000s R&B boy band B2K. To further push her into the limelight, she was marketed as the cousin of one of the women on B2K Boys (Lil’ Fizz), while this was false. This was a step to generate excitement for her planned debut album in 2003, entitled My Name Is Jhené. However, this album was not released because the singer canceled her contract with her record label Sony as she wanted to continue her education.

Jhené Aiko – short biography, age

Jhené Aiko Efuru Chilombo was born in Los Angeles, California on March 16, 1988. She comes from a middle-class family consisting of her father, Karamo Chilombo, her mother, Christina Yamamoto, and her four siblings. Growing up was a bittersweet experience for her. She has revealed that she still has memories of the horrific 1992 incident that saw Rodney King police brutality. This mistreatment led to an uprising in solidarity with the victim. Unfortunately, the violent riots resulted in the deaths of over 50 people and 2,000 injuries. Also, as a child, Jhené did not have a pleasant experience at school because she was constantly teased about her appearance, which did not fall into the popular racial classifications.

Still, the wonderful part of her childhood was the music. Whenever she was sad, music was her concern to take her time and comfort. She began taking music and singing lessons at the age of four. To make things more interesting, her older sisters Jamila and Miyoko were already in the limelight with an R&B music group called “Gyrl”. Through her sisters, she had the opportunity to introduce her singing skills to then-producer Chris Stokes at the age of five. By the time she was seven, Jhené Aiko had written quite a few rap songs.

music career

She has always been influenced by music as her older sisters were already successful singers. She later became part of the R&B boy group, B2K, and signed with Epic Music when she was just twelve years old. She was also featured in the group’s debut video album, Uhhh , in 2002. Surprisingly, the video won the BET Award for Viewer’s Choice. In 2003, Jhené Aiko attempted to release her debut album (My Name is Jhene) through record labels such as Sony, The Ultimate Group and Epic. However, it didn’t work out and the album was never released as a result of differences faced between the labels and themselves.

Even more, she had a strong desire to pursue hereducation, so she decided to get out of business. After completing her studies, she returned to the music scene in 2011 with a mixtape entitled “Sailing Souls”. Among them were actors like Drake, Miguel and Kanye West. Following the success of the mixtape and a growing fan base, she was signed to Artium Record Imprints through Def Jam the next year. Her first EP, “Sail Out,” was released in November 2013 and it did so well that it reached #1 on the R&B chart. Her follow-up album “Trip” was released in 2017.

Parents, ethnicity, siblings

It’s pretty obvious that Aiko doesn’t fit any popular racial classification perfectly and the reason is simply that she’s of mixed heritage. Her father, Karamo Chilombo, a pediatrician, is African American, Native American, Jewish and German. On the other hand, her mother, Christina Yamamoto, also has a mix of Japanese, Dominican, and Spanish heritage.

She also has four siblings, two sisters (JamilaAkiko Mila Chilombo and Miyoko Chilombo) and two brothers (Miyagi Hasani Ayo Chilombo and Jahi Chilombo). Sadly, her brother Miyagi Hasani passed away on April 19, 2012 after losing a battle with cancer. Before his death, Aiko wrote a song for him called “For My Brother”. Shortly after hearing it, he gave up the ghost. She also has three other half-siblings from her father’s side. Their names are; Dio Chilombo, Kareena Chilombo and Marcia Chilombo.

Personal life – married, husband, daughter

Jhené Aiko is every inch a gorgeous lady who has had a good number of relationships since her popularity grew in her chosen field. In 2005, she began dating prolific R&B singer O’Ryan, and they both welcomed a daughter named Namiko Love on November 19, 2008. That same year her daughter was born, she and her boyfriend O’Ryan called it ending their relationship.

After the split, the couple was still very much on good terms and they traded co-parenting. A major tragedy was averted on August 27, 2013 after she, her older sister Miyoko, their daughter Namiko and ex-boyfriend O’Ryan were involved in a car accident. Luckily no life was lost and she alone had broken a wrist and had to sew up her chin.

On March 16, 2016, news broke that the star had secretly married her husband, Oladipo “Dot da Genius” Omishore. The couple began dating in late 2015. Unfortunately, that marriage didn’t last a year as Aiko filed for divorce and found irreconcilable differences. The marriage was finally dissolved in October 2017. Aiko and Big Sean are currently dating after splitting from her husband Oladipo.
