Who is Eva Longoria?

Eva Longoria is a talented and versatile American actress, producer, and activist. Born on March 15, 1975, in Corpus Christi, Texas, Longoria rose to fame in the early 2000s for her role as Gabrielle Solis on the hit TV series, “Desperate Housewives.”

Longoria’s acting career began in the late 1990s, with minor roles in various TV shows and movies. However, it was her breakout role as Gabrielle Solis on “Desperate Housewives” that launched her into the mainstream. The show, which ran from 2004 to 2012, made Longoria a household name and earned her numerous award nominations, including a Golden Globe.

After “Desperate Housewives,” Longoria continued to work in television, appearing in shows like “Telenovela” and “Grand Hotel.” She also expanded her work behind the camera, producing shows like “Devious Maids” and “Grand Hotel.”

Longoria has also been active in philanthropy and activism throughout her career. She founded the Eva Longoria Foundation in 2012, which aims to empower Latinas through education and entrepreneurship. She has also been an outspoken advocate for various social and political issues, including immigration reform, women’s rights, and the LGBTQ+ community.

In addition to her acting and philanthropic work, Longoria is also known for her fashion sense and beauty. She has been a spokesperson for various beauty brands, and in 2017, she launched her own fashion line, the Eva Longoria Collection.
