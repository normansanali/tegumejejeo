Art Bell – Biography

Art Bell is an American broadcaster and novelist who is recognized as “The King of Late Night Radio.” In addition, Art Bell founded and aired the paranormal radio show Coast to Coast AM. His show was also heard on hundreds of radio stations throughout the United States and Canada. Dreamland, the show’s companion, was likewise created and presented by him.

Art Bell – Professional Career

During the Vietnam War, Art Bell worked as a physician in the ‘US Air Force,’ where he and a friend launched an illegal pirate radio station transmitting anti-war songs from the ‘Amarillo Air Force Base.’ After the war, he moved to Japan and worked as a DJ for ‘KSBK,’ the only civil English radio station in the country at the time. He moved to the United States in the 1970s and worked for ‘KIDD’ in Monterey, California.

Art Bell started as a rock music DJ before transitioning to hosting radio talk shows. He proceeded beyond conventional political concerns to more problematic topics including gunrunning, the occult, UFOs, pseudoscience, conspiracy theories, and protoscience with his talk show ‘Dreamland.’

Art Bell founded KNYE 95.1 FM, a Pahrump-based radio station with a broadcasting studio and transmitter near his house in Nye County, Nevada. His show ‘Coast to Coast AM,’ which he created in his studio, became one of the most popular radio shows in the late 1980s and early 1990s. It was broadcast on over 500 stations and over 15 million people tuned in to hear it.

Art Bell established credibility through hosting live question-and-answer sessions on his show. He honed the skill of patiently listening and then responding objectively. Art Bell only screened questions once, in October 2006, when he broadcasted from the Philippines. He spoke with singers, authors, actors, DJs, and comedians, among others. Over the course of his illustrious career, he interviewed notable figures such as Willie Nelson, Merle Haggard, Greg Bear, Chris Carter, Dan Aykroyd, Casey Kasem, and George Carlin. His talks were well-received because they were laid-back while still covering significant and topical topics.
