Is nitric acid soluble in oil?

Oils, on the other hand, are non-polar; their molecules have the same charge all the way around. Water easily dissolves other polar substances, like sodium chloride salt, but does not dissolve non-polar molecules such as oil. For the same reason, acids, which are polar molecules, generally do not dissolve oil.

What type of compound is nitric acid?

Nitric acid is a nitrogen oxoacid of formula HNO3 in which the nitrogen atom is bonded to a hydroxy group and by equivalent bonds to the remaining two oxygen atoms.

What is the fuming of nitric acid?

a colorless, yellowish, or brownish fuming corrosive liquid, usually prepared from nitric acid by the addition of excess nitrogen dioxide: used in organic synthesis for nitration, and as an oxidizer in liquid propellants for rockets.

Why is nitric acid called Aqua Fortis?

Nitric acid is known as aqua fortis. Aqua means water and fortis means strong. … Nitric acid has the molecular formula $HN{O_3}$ , it is called Aqua fortis because it looks like water and has the same viscosity as water. It can also react with metals like silver.

Is nitric acid organic or inorganic?

Commonly used inorganic acids are sulfuric acid (H2SO4), hydrochloric acid (HCl), and nitric acid (HNO3). Inorganic acids range from superacids (such as perchloric acid, HClO4) to very weak acids (such as boric acid, H3BO3). Inorganic acids tend to be very soluble in water and insoluble in organic solvents.

How do you make 5% HNO3?

The volume of 7.14 g of concentrated HNO3 can be obtained from the density V = 7.14 g / 1.42 = 5.03 mL. If you then measure 5.03 mL of the concentrated HNO3 and dilute to 100 mL with water, you would have the required 5% solution.
