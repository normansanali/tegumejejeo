He attended Long Beach City College, where he studied music theory and composition. In 1971, Rhodes joined the Doobie Brothers, and he remained with the band until 1982. During his time with the band, he wrote or co-wrote several of their hit songs, including "Black Water," "China Grove," and "Takin' It to the Streets." He also sang lead vocals on several of their songs, including "South City Midnight Lady" and "What a Fool Believes." In 1982, Rhodes left the Doobie Brothers to pursue a solo career. He released his first solo album, Tell the Truth, in 1983. He has since released several more solo albums, including Southern Pacific (1984), Street Talk (1986), and Michael Rhodes (1989). Rhodes has also worked as a session musician, playing on albums by artists such as Bob Seger, Bonnie Raitt, and Kenny Loggins. In 2016, Rhodes won Bass Player of the Year as awarded by the Academy of Country Music.
